RocketConf 2015

A three-day single track conference for modern web developers

11, 12 and 13 November, 2015 — Amsterdam, The Netherlands

Awesome! You’re here!

This November we will bring you RocketConf, a three-day developer conference for the modern web developer and dev ops who loves writing beautiful code and creating magnificent things that matter.

RocketConf does not focus on any particual technology or tool. We believe that you can learn more from people with a different perspective. Technical topics can range from Ruby on Rails to Ember.js to Elixir to Docker and everything in between.

To learn something well, you should get hands-on experience. On the 11th November we'll host workshops where you can get together with others and get a real learning experience by doing something fun. The 12th and 13th November are reserved for the conference talks.

Talking tech in beautiful Amsterdam

Speakers

More speakers will be announced in the coming months!

  • Dr. André Kuipers

    Astronaut & Ambassador of Earth

    André Kuipers is the first Dutchman with two space missions to his name. He offers a unique look behind the scenes of international human spaceflight.

  • Corey Haines

    Software Journeyman

    Corey Haines is an author, speaker, teacher and all around groovy guy. While his true contribution to the world is an undying love of cats, he also co-created and led the evolution of the Coderetreat workshop.

  • Kevlin Henney

    Consultant, speaker, writer and trainer

    Kevlin is an independent consultant, speaker, writer and trainer. His development interests are in patterns, programming, practice and process. He has been a columnist for various magazines and web sites, and been involved in more committees than can be considered healthy.

  • Francesc Campoy

    Go Developer Programs Engineer

    Francesc Campoy joined the Go team in 2012 as Developer Programs Engineer. Since then, he has written some considerable didactic resources and traveled the world attending conferences and organizing live courses.

  • Jessie Frazelle

    Core Docker maintainer

    Jessie Frazelle works on the Core Team at Docker, maintaining the docker/docker project. She enjoys hacking everything possible into containers and scaling out the Docker CI and tools. She loves all things Linux.

  • Mark Bates

    Software Developer, Author, Father, Entrepreneur

    Mark Bates is the founder and chief architect of the Boston, MA based consulting company, Meta42 Labs. Mark spends his days focusing on new application development and consulting for his clients. At night he writes books, raises kids, and occasionally he forms a band and "tries to make it".

Call for proposals

We like to think about the modern web in the broadest possible way. How it works today, how it affects lives and how it shapes the future. And we'd love to hear your ideas on it.

RocketConf offers you a stage to share your story with your peers. This is a great opportunity to share and give back to the community. Please submit your proposal for a talk or workshop before 31 July 2015. Do it today!

Submit a proposal

In the light of our Code of Conduct we want to give everyone a fair chance of speaking at RocketConf. Submissions will be anonymized before being handed over to the programme team, who will make a selection based on the content of your proposal.

Tickets

For the early birds we have a limited supply of tickets available at a discounted price. Early bird tickets will be available as long as the supply lasts until July 31.

Get your ticket now!

There are two types of tickets available to RocketConf 2015. The Workshops + Conference tickets will give you access to the workshops on 11 November and the two conference days. The Conference tickets will give you access to both conference days only.
